Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1917 by S.B. & Co. of Chester; grossed 5,672 tons and measured 122.12 x 16.53 x 9.55 metres.
  • Powered by a single screw triple-expansion engine.
  • Bound New York for Avonmouth with petroleum when mined (mine laid by UC-43) 20 miles off Fastnet Rock.
  • Escort "HMS Zinna" returned and attempted a tow, but the vessel sank before reaching land.
  • Baroness de Silva took photos of the bow remaining above water for over an hour after the incident.
  • Diving: The wreck sits upright, 3 miles South of Baltimore Beacon, and remains largely intact.
