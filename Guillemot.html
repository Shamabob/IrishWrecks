Guillemot LV (No. 514c)

Last update - 23-Oct-2020
Vessel Type: Light Vessel Location: Co. Wicklow
Date of Loss: 28th March 1917 Place: Arklow Bank
Cause of Loss: Bombed by U-Boat Boat Dive from: Arklow Quay
Charted Depth: 53m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 62
Height of Wreck: 7m Admiralty Chart No: 1787 and/or 1468
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (approx.): 52° 40' 16.02" North
Type of Seabed: Sand Longitude (approx.): 05° 57' 33.17" West
Average Visibility: 3 - 8m Diving Experience: Experienced
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built of steel and wood, she measured 96 x 22 x 12 feet.
  • Boarded by the crew of UC-65 (commanded by Captain Otto Steinbrinck); explosives were placed onboard.
  • The crew escaped before the charges detonated; as explosives failed to sink her, UC-65 shelled the vessel until it sank.
  • Diving: Lies upright close to the South Arklow marker buoy with damage to her port side.
  • Visibility is usually very poor due to large amounts of sand moved by tides; consultations with local knowledge and permission from Irish Lights are advised.
