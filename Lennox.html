Lennox SS (No. 557)

Last update - 05-Nov-2005
Vessel Type: Cargo Steamer Location: Co. Wexford
Date of Loss: 18th January 1917 Place: Great Saltee
Cause of Loss: Struck rocks Boat Dive from: Kilmore Quay
Charted Depth: 10m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 77
Height of Wreck: 5m Admiralty Chart No: 2740 and/or 1410
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (GPS): 52° 06' 47.34" North
Type of Seabed: Gravel Longitude (GPS): 06° 36' 45.42" West
Average Visibility: 10 - 15m Diving Experience: Novice
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1895 by Barclay Curle & Co. Glasgow; grossed 3,677 tons and measured 107.44 x 13.46 x 7.72m.
  • Powered by a 3 cylinder triple expansion engine.
  • Chased by a U-boat onto the rocks at Great Saltee.
  • The Kilmore Lifeboat successfully rescued the crew of 45.
  • Diving: Lies parallel to the Collough Rocks behind Great Saltee; extensively broken up.
  • The boilers are the highest point of the wreck.
  • Subject to slight currents. Launch from the slip at Kilmore Quay.
