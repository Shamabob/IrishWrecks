Last update - 04-Apr-2003

Average Visibility: 8 - 10m Diving Experience: Experienced Novice
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1911, she measured 43.71 x 7.41 x 3.17m and grossed 426 tons.
  • Owned by S.W. Coe & Co. Ltd., Liverpool, she ran onto Saddle Rock in fog while bound for Larne with general cargo.
  • After unsuccessful attempts to free her, she was sold to John Lee & Co.
  • On 12th December 1955, she slipped off the rock and sank during heavy seas.
  • The wreck lies on the South-East tip of Saddle Rock and is badly broken up; artifacts can still be found.
  • Best dived 30 minutes before High or Low Water at Belfast, with the end of the ebb tide being ideal.
