Gavina SFV (No. 480)

Last update - 25-Oct-2005
Vessel Type: Steam Trawler Location: Co. Antrim
Date of Loss: 5th October 1921 Place: Rathlin Island
Cause of Loss: Ran ashore Boat Dive from: Ballycastle
Charted Depth: 6m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 5
Height of Wreck: Scattered Admiralty Chart No: 2798
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (approx.): 55° 17' 50" North
Type of Seabed: Rocks and kelp Longitude (approx.): 06° 10' 00" West
Average Visibility: 10 - 15m Diving Experience: Experienced Novice
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1916 by Cochrane & Sons, Selby; grossed 289 tons and served as a Royal Navy minesweeper during the war.
  • Ran onto a reef in dense fog North of Bruce's Castle while bound for fishing grounds from Fleetwood.
  • Unsuccessful salvage attempt; islanders removed much of the vessel, including a lifeboat used locally for years.
  • Wreckage lies 300m North of Bruce's Castle; boiler visible just under the surface at 6m depth.
  • Dive on flood tide due to strong ebb (5 knots). Fee applies for Ballycastle slipway usage.
