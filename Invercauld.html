Invercauld SV (No. 542a)

Last update - 02-Feb-2014
Vessel Type: Three Masted Barque Location: Co. Wexford
Date of Loss: 22nd February 1917 Place: Coningbeg Rock
Cause of Loss: Torpedoed Boat Dive from: Kilmore Quay
Charted Depth: 45m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 77
Height of Wreck: 2m Admiralty Chart No: 2740 and/or 1410
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (GPS): 52° 04' 09" North
Type of Seabed: Rocks & gravel Longitude (GPS): 06° 38' 28.8" West
Average Visibility: 10 - 15m Diving Experience: Experienced
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1891 by A. McMillan & Sons, Dumbarton; grossed 1,416 tons and measured 300ft. long.
  • Owned by Marine Nav. Co. and carried a cargo of timber.
  • Initially shelled by U-84, she was finally torpedoed off Mine Head and drifted until sinking after striking Coningbeg Rock.
  • All aboard were rescued, and the wreck was identified by her bell recovered in the 1990's.
  • Diving: Lies half a mile east of Coningbeg Rock, south of the Saltee Islands; sitting upright but extensively broken up.
  • Subject to strong currents outside slack water. Launch from the slip at Kilmore Quay.
