Polewell SS (No. 657)

Last update - 08-Jul-2020
Vessel Type: Steam Collier Location: Co. Dublin
Date of Loss: 5th June 1917 Place: Lambay Island
Cause of Loss: Torpedoed Boat Dive from: Howth Harbour
Charted Depth: 36m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 50
Height of Wreck: 6m Admiralty Chart No: 1468 and/or 44
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (approx.): 53° 33' 02" North
Type of Seabed: Silt Longitude (approx.): 05° 55' 03" West
Average Visibility: 6 - 10m Diving Experience: Experienced
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1888 by J. L. Thompson & Son, Sunderland, measuring 86.56 x 11.60 x 5.53m with a gross tonnage of 2,013.
  • Powered by a 3 cylinder triple-expansion steam engine generating 201 h.p.
  • Torpedoed by U-96 while bound for France from Troon with a cargo of coal.
  • The wreck lies 3 miles North-East of Lambay Island, lying upright but starting to break up.
  • Diving features include portholes and large chunks of coal, best dived at slack high.
  • Warning: Numerous nets are snagged on the wreck.
  • Note: Owned by Nautilus SAC; permission to dive is required.
