Bouncer SS (No. 120)

Last update - 03-Apr-2006
Vessel Type: Salvage Ship Location: Co. Antrim
Date of Loss: 28th September 1921 Place: Rathlin Island
Cause of Loss: Struck Rocks Boat Dive from: Ballycastle
Charted Depth: 8m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 5
Height of Wreck: Scattered Admiralty Chart No: 2798
Hull Material: Steel Latitude (approx.): 55° 17' 30" North
Type of Seabed: Boulders and kelp Longitude (approx.): 06° 17' 00" West
Average Visibility: 5 - 10m Diving Experience: Novice
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Built in 1879 by H.M. Dockyard, Pembroke; owned by North of Ireland Marine Salvage Co.
  • Served as a submarine tender, supply boat, and gunboat; was involved in the relief of General Gordon on the Nile.
  • Struck rocks at Tolovney while salvaged anchor cables snapped during a ceilidh; no lives were lost.
  • Lies at the same site as the "Knights Gareth" with wreckage scattered; largest object is a boiler.
  • Her steel propeller is located in a nearby cave. Area has little to no current.
