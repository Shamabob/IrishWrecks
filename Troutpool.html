Troutpool SS (No. 850)

Last update - 08-Apr-2008
Vessel Type: Cargo Steamer Location: Co. Down
Date of Loss: 20th July 1940 Place: Bangor
Cause of Loss: Mined Boat Dive from: Bangor
Charted Depth: 15m Irish O.S. Map: Discovery Series No. 15
Height of Wreck: Scattered Admiralty Chart No: 1753
Hull Material: Steel/Iron Latitude (GPS): 54° 41' 51.6" North
Type of Seabed: Sandy Longitude (GPS): 05° 40' 23.16" West
Average Visibility: 3 - 8m Diving Experience: Experienced Novice
Historical & Diving Notes
  • Owned by Pool Shipping Co. and built in 1927 by W. Gray & Co.
  • Measured 390.0 x 55.0 x 26.4 feet; grossed 4,886 tons.
  • On a voyage from Rosario to Glasgow with 7,908 tons of grain when she struck a mine and sank off Bangor Pier Light.
  • The sinking resulted in the loss of 11 lives.
  • The wreck was later blown up and dispersed because it was considered a hazard to other shipping.
  • Diving: The site is now mainly scattered plates lying directly off Bangor Pier Light with the bow pointing South-West.
  • Can be dived at almost any state of the tide, though 1 hour before High Water Belfast is recommended.
